Research Project
Dec. 2022 - Apr. 2023
Aim:
1) establish a connection between reputation and economic benefits to foster positive market development.
2) break the monopoly and create space for new businesses to thrive.
Contribution:
(1) propose a blockchain-based reputation evaluation mechanism that dynamically assesses the reputation of both transaction parties;
(2) establish a connection between personal gains and reputation during the negotiation process;
(3) propose a mechanism to protect novices and mitigate market monopolies.
Agent-based Personalized Assessment Task Recommendation Considering Multiple Factors
Graduate's Degree Thesis
Dec. 2021 - Oct. 2022
Aim:
accurately recommend personalized assessment tasks for learners, taking into account learning ability, academic emotion, academic expectations of learners and teachers.
Contribution:
(1) adaptively recommend content of assessment tasks based on changes in objective factors.
(2) use an autonomous-agent-based approach that considers subjective factors to recommend assessment tasks.
(3) propose an algorithm that balances relationships between objective and subjective factors, addressing the cold start problem when recommendation.
A Self-Supervised Tree-Structured Framework for Fine-Grained Classification
Research Project
Jun. 2022 - Dec. 2022
Aim:
propose a self-supervised tree-structured classifier by eliminating the effect of differences between classes in feature extraction to improve classification accuracy of fine-grained classification.
Contribution:
(1) a self-supervised method that creates a classification tree, eliminating the need for manual labeling;
(2) a machine-learning matcher which determines the belonging cluster, minimizing the impact of inter-cluster variations on classification;
(3) a pruning criterion which filters the tree-structured classifier, retaining only models with superior classification performance.
Personalized Lane Changing Guidance System for Network-connected Vehicles
Bachelor's Degree Thesis
Dec. 2019 - Jun. 2021
Aim:
establish a connection between car-following and lane-changing behaviors, enabling the personalized provision of personalized and safe strategies for lane-changing.
Contribution:
(1) establish a connection between car-following and lane-changing behaviors;
(2) based on the extracted lane-changing characteristics, drivers are classified as radical type, ordinary type, and novice type;
(3) based on the extracted lane change characteristics, personalized lane change strategies are provided.
